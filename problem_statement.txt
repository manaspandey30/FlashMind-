Problem 1: The Flashcard Engine Context: One of Cuemath's core beliefs is that long-term retention beats short-term cramming. Spaced repetition and active recall are the two most effective study techniques known to cognitive science. Yet most students still study by passively re-reading notes. Build: A flashcard app that turns any PDF into a smart, practice-ready deck of flashcards. A student drops in a PDF — a chapter on quadratic equations, class notes on the French Revolution — and gets back a clean set of cards ready to practice. But the real magic isn't in the conversion. It's what happens after. Think about: ● Ingestion quality. A lazy approach gives you 10 shallow cards. A great approach gives you cards that cover the material comprehensively — key concepts, definitions, relationships, edge cases, worked examples. Cards that feel like they were written by a great teacher, not scraped by a bot. ● Spaced repetition. Cards you know well should fade. Cards you struggle with keep showing up. Look into SM-2, Leitner boxes, or invent your own. The app should be smart about what it shows you and when. ● Progress and mastery. The student should feel progress. Show what they've mastered, what's shaky, what's coming up for review. Motivating, not overwhelming. ● Deck management. Over time, dozens of decks. Make it easy to browse, search, revisit, pick up where you left off. ● Delight. Flashcard apps are notoriously boring. Yours doesn't have to be.
